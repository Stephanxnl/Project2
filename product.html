Story

Electronic Textiles or more commonly referred to as E-Textiles are modern fabrics infused with electronic components. Similar to smart fabrics or novel fabrics, these are further explored in the hopes of providing added value to clothing. E-textiles and functional fabrics are designed with embedded sensors, microcontrollers, batteries, and various other smart electronics. These are used to convert plain clothes or accessories into fully functional but still aesthetically pleasing garments. With all the tech infusions, these wearables and apparel can detect and respond to external factors or environmental conditions. And with all the added functionalities and uses, these sustainable smart clothes are not just eco-friendly but also lifesavers. Smart fabrics are taking convenience to a whole new level. These tech innovations are showcasing various uses and benefits.

How to use

Garment innovations aren’t merely affecting the fashion industry but various other industries at the same time. Gamer vests, for instance, have some sort of components that can simulate real-life combat. These make you feel like you’ve physically taken a hit when you get shot in your game, for example. Smart fabrics aren’t just shallow or meaningless innovations. In fact, various fashion brands from all over the world have started adopting and embracing these changes.

Who is using

Some e-textiles are specifically designed for athletes or those whose lifestyles involve active and/or continuous movement. For instance, there are running shorts that can capture the technicalities of their performances, including speed and distance, as well as the player’s running technique used.

Future

Technology, indeed, always finds ways in improving various aspects of the world and our lives. And the fashion industry is of no exemption. With continuous innovations, there’s no denying the bright future of the smart clothing industry. In fact, some experts foresee it to be a multi-billion dollar industry by 2025.
